The Insight

All urban gardens hold the potential to unlock real ecological value — habitat for native species, cooler streets, healthier soil, canopy cover.

But without measurement, recognition, and support, that potential remains unrealised.

YIELD changes that.

What “Yield” Means

Yield is the annual return you receive when the ecological outcomes your garden delivers are supported by sponsors.

It’s not an offset. It’s not a donation.

It’s recognition of value you’re already creating — and an incentive to create more.

Sponsors fund gardens that deliver real, local ecological outcomes. You receive that support, year after year, as long as those outcomes continue.

How It Works

1. Discover

Find out what ecological value your garden already produces.

2. Improve

Work with your garden to increase habitat, canopy, or pollinator support — at your own pace.

3. Activate

Choose to make your garden’s outcomes available to sponsors seeking local ecological impact.

4. Receive

Get annual income or in-kind support while your garden continues to perform.

What Can Generate Yield

Habitat

Indigenous plants that support local bird, insect, and animal species.

Pollinators

Flowering natives that sustain bees, butterflies, and other essential species.

Canopy

Trees and shrubs that provide shade, cooling, and carbon storage.

Cooling

Vegetation that reduces urban heat and improves neighbourhood climate.

Soil

Healthy, living ground cover that supports water retention and biodiversity below the surface.

For Sponsors

Trust & Verification

Outcomes are measured against Ecological Registry standards and recorded annually.

Your address and personal details remain private. Participation is voluntary and can be paused or ended at any time.

You decide what information is shared, and with whom.
